## 1. Slow Motion Techniques

### Research Findings

**Source:** [AI-Powered Video Guide 2025 - ReelMind](https://reelmind.ai/blog/ai-powered-video-the-definitive-prompting-guide-for-2025)

> "Slow motion is frequently recommended for emphasizing drama or beauty in video generations. Control the energy of your video: 'Slow motion' - emphasize drama or beauty"

**Best Practice Example:**
```
"Fresh strawberry being dipped into rich melted chocolate, chocolate dripping off
in slow motion, dark moody background, dramatic side lighting highlighting texture,
close-up macro shot, shallow depth of field"
```

### Effective Slow Motion Keywords (2025)

**Primary Keywords:**
- `"slow motion"` - General slow-mo effect
- `"dramatic slow motion"` - Emphasizes drama
- `"extreme slow motion"` - Ultra-slow, high detail
- `"120fps slow motion"` - Technical specification
- `"time dilation effect"` - Cinematic slow-down

**Pairing Recommendations:**
- Slow motion + Macro close-up = Maximum detail capture
- Slow motion + Backlighting = Dramatic particle effects
- Slow motion + Shallow DOF = Cinematic product shots

### Application by Genre

**Action Commercials:**
```
"Athlete diving into water in extreme slow motion, water droplets suspended in air,
backlit by golden hour sun, Canon 5D with 120fps, shallow depth of field"
```

**Product Showcases:**
```
"Luxury watch rotating in slow motion, close-up macro, golden highlights catching
on polished metal, black velvet background, studio lighting"
```

**Emotional Storytelling:**
```
"Child running through meadow in slow motion, arms outstretched, sunset backlight
creating lens flares, dreamy atmosphere, Arri Alexa 65"
```

## 2. Close-Up & Macro Cinematography

### Research Findings

**Source:** [Google Veo 3 Prompt Examples - Skywork.ai](https://skywork.ai/blog/google-veo-3-prompt-examples-2025/)

> "An extreme close-up of a single, glistening drop of rain as it lands on the petal
> of a vibrant red rose, causing the petal to tremble slightly"

**Source:** [Veo on Vertex AI Prompt Guide - Google Cloud](https://docs.cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/generative-ai/docs/video/video-gen-prompt-guide)

> "The camera starts with a macro close-up of a glowing jellyfish and then expands
> to reveal the entire ocean lit up with various glowing colors under a starry sky"

### Close-Up Shot Hierarchy

**Medium Close-Up (MCU):**
- Framing: Chest to top of head
- Purpose: Dialogue, emotion, connection
- Keywords: `"medium close-up"`, `"MCU"`, `"chest up"`

**Close-Up (CU):**
- Framing: Shoulders to top of head
- Purpose: Emotion, detail, intimacy
- Keywords: `"close-up"`, `"CU"`, `"tight on face"`

**Extreme Close-Up (ECU):**
- Framing: Eyes, lips, hands, small details
- Purpose: Maximum emotion, product detail
- Keywords: `"extreme close-up"`, `"ECU"`, `"macro shot"`

**Macro Cinematography:**
- Framing: Microscopic detail, textures
- Purpose: Product shots, nature, abstract
- Keywords: `"macro"`, `"macro lens"`, `"100mm macro"`, `"ultra close detail"`

### Professional Macro Prompts (2025)

**Food & Beverage:**
```
"Macro close-up of cold soda poured into glass with ice, slow motion bubbles rising,
effervescent sparkle, backlit amber highlights, condensation beads on glass,
shallow depth of field, studio lighting"
```

**Product Detail:**
```
"Extreme close-up of smartphone camera lens, light reflecting off sapphire crystal,
macro detail showing precision engineering, black matte aluminum body,
dramatic side lighting, shallow DOF, premium aesthetic"
```

**Nature:**
```
"Macro close-up of single water droplet on green leaf, morning dew,
soft diffused natural light, ultra shallow depth of field, droplet reflects
entire forest scene, 100mm macro lens aesthetic"
```

**Texture Emphasis:**
```
"Extreme close-up of leather watch strap texture, grain detail,
warm side lighting emphasizing ridges, macro lens, shallow depth of field,
brown tones, luxury craftsmanship visible"
```

## 3. Dynamic Lighting Techniques

### Research Findings

**Source:** [Veo 3 Prompting Best Practices - Superprompt.com](https://superprompt.com/blog/veo3-prompting-best-practices)

> "Professional three-point lighting combines a warm key light from camera left,
> subtle fill light to soften shadows, and rim lighting to separate subject
> from background"

**Source:** [Mastering Veo 3 Camera Control - Medium](https://medium.com/@miguelivanov/mastering-veo-3-an-expert-guide-to-optimal-prompt-structure-and-cinematic-camera-control-693d01ae9f8b)

> "Color temperature significantly impacts emotional tone, such as 'warm tungsten
> lighting (3200K) in the cozy cabin interior contrasting with cool daylight (5600K)'"

### Professional Lighting Setups

#### 3-Point Lighting (Standard)
```
"Three-point lighting setup: warm key light from camera left, soft fill light
from camera right reducing shadows, rim light from behind separating subject
from background, professional studio aesthetic"
```

**When to Use:** Corporate videos, interviews, product showcases, professional tone

#### Rembrandt Lighting (Dramatic)
```
"Rembrandt lighting: single key light positioned 45 degrees camera left,
creating signature triangle of light on shadow-side cheek, dramatic chiaroscuro,
deep shadows adding mystery and sophistication"
```

**When to Use:** Luxury products, dramatic storytelling, portrait-style commercials

#### Film Noir Lighting (High Contrast)
```
"Film noir cinematography: dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, deep shadows engulfing
half the frame, venetian blind light patterns, stark highlights, high contrast
black and white aesthetic, mystery and tension"
```

**When to Use:** Thriller-style commercials, edgy brands, mysterious products

#### High-Key Lighting (Bright & Cheerful)
```
"High-key lighting: bright, even illumination, minimal shadows, soft diffused light,
cheerful and uplifting mood, white background, clean and professional aesthetic"
```

**When to Use:** Healthcare, family products, cheerful brands, optimistic messaging

#### Low-Key Lighting (Dark & Moody)
```
"Low-key lighting: single light source, predominantly dark frame, selective
illumination on subject, deep shadows, mysterious and dramatic mood,
film noir inspiration"
```

**When to Use:** Alcohol, automotive, premium tech, nightlife brands

### Natural Lighting Techniques

#### Golden Hour (Warm & Romantic)
```
"Golden hour lighting: warm sunset glow (3200K color temperature), long soft shadows,
backlit subject with rim lighting, lens flares, romantic and nostalgic atmosphere,
natural film grain"
```

**When to Use:** Travel, romance, lifestyle, outdoor products

#### Blue Hour (Cool & Mysterious)
```
"Blue hour lighting: cool twilight tones (6500K), deep blue sky, artificial lights
glowing warm contrast, mysterious and cinematic mood, natural gradient from
blue to amber"
```

**When to Use:** Urban products, nightlife, tech, sophisticated brands

#### Overcast Diffused (Soft & Professional)
```
"Overcast natural lighting: soft diffused light, no harsh shadows, even illumination,
professional and clean aesthetic, gentle gradients, color accuracy maintained"
```

**When to Use:** Fashion, product photography, testimonials, clean aesthetic

### Artificial Lighting Effects

#### Neon Glow (Urban & Edgy)
```
"Pulsating neon lighting: vibrant pink and cyan neon signs reflecting on wet
pavement, urban cyberpunk aesthetic, high contrast colors, atmospheric fog,
nighttime city mood"
```

**When to Use:** Tech products, urban brands, gaming, nightlife

#### Firelight (Warm & Intimate)
```
"Flickering firelight illumination: warm amber glow (2000K), dancing shadows,
intimate and cozy atmosphere, natural movement, rustic aesthetic"
```

**When to Use:** Home products, comfort brands, winter campaigns, intimate settings

#### Studio Strobes (Professional & Clean)
```
"Studio strobe lighting: multiple softboxes creating even illumination,
white seamless background, no color cast, professional product photography aesthetic,
crisp highlights"
```

**When to Use:** E-commerce, product launches, clean modern brands

### Color Temperature Guide (2025)

**Warm Tones (Emotional, Cozy):**
- Candlelight: 1800K-2000K
- Tungsten/Incandescent: 2700K-3200K
- Golden Hour: 3200K-3500K

**Neutral Tones (Professional, Balanced):**
- Daylight: 5000K-5600K
- Flash: 5500K
- Overcast Sky: 6500K-7500K

**Cool Tones (Tech, Modern):**
- Cloudy Sky: 7000K-8000K
- Blue Hour: 10000K-12000K
- Deep Blue Sky: 12000K+

## 4. Camera Angles & Movements

### Research Findings

**Source:** [Runway Gen-4 Prompting Guide](https://help.runwayml.com/hc/en-us/articles/39789879462419-Gen-4-Video-Prompting-Guide)

> "Pan shots, tracking shots, and dolly movements have a more cinematic quality,
> with the model better understanding the physics of how cameras move through space"

**Source:** [12 Essential Camera Movements for AI Video - LetsEnhance](https://letsenhance.io/blog/all/ai-video-camera-movements/)

> "AI video generators can create dolly shots where the camera physically moves
> toward the subject, creating intimacy and changing perspective so the background
> 'wraps' around the subject"

### Camera Angles (Static Framing)

#### Eye-Level (Neutral, Documentary)
```
"Eye-level camera angle, neutral perspective, documentary style, natural viewpoint,
subject at same height as viewer"
```
**Psychology:** Equality, neutrality, objectivity
**Use Case:** Interviews, testimonials, neutral storytelling

#### Low Angle (Powerful, Heroic)
```
"Low angle camera looking up at subject, heroic framing, subject appears powerful
and dominant, dramatic sky background, wide-angle distortion"
```
**Psychology:** Power, dominance, heroism, authority
**Use Case:** Leadership messaging, empowerment, product superiority

#### High Angle (Vulnerable, Overview)
```
"High angle camera looking down at subject, bird's eye perspective, subject appears
smaller and more vulnerable, environmental context visible"
```
**Psychology:** Vulnerability, overview, context, humility
**Use Case:** Storytelling vulnerability, establishing shots, context

#### Dutch Angle (Tension, Unease)
```
"Dutch angle (canted angle), camera tilted 15 degrees, horizon line diagonal,
creates tension and unease, dynamic composition, off-kilter framing"
```
**Psychology:** Tension, chaos, unease, dynamism
**Use Case:** Action sequences, disorientation, edgy brands

#### Over-the-Shoulder (Dialogue, POV)
```
"Over-the-shoulder angle, shoulder and back of head in foreground, subject visible
in mid-ground, depth and spatial relationship, dialogue scene aesthetic"
```
**Psychology:** Conversation, relationship, perspective
**Use Case:** Dialogue scenes, human connection, storytelling

### Camera Movements (Dynamic)

#### Dolly In (Intimacy, Focus)
```
"Smooth dolly-in movement, camera physically moves forward toward subject,
background perspective shifts, creates intimacy and draws viewer attention,
steadicam aesthetic, gradual reveal"
```
**When to Use:** Emphasizing emotion, product reveal, building tension
**Speed:** Slow (dramatic) or Fast (energetic)

#### Dolly Out (Context, Reveal)
```
"Dolly-out camera movement, starting close on subject then pulling back to reveal
environment, expanding perspective, contextual reveal, smooth backward motion"
```
**When to Use:** Establishing scale, context reveal, ending shots
**Speed:** Medium to slow for dramatic effect

#### Tracking Shot (Following Action)
```
"Lateral tracking shot, camera smoothly follows subject moving through space,
maintains consistent distance, dynamic movement, Steadicam aesthetic,
fluid motion parallax"
```
**When to Use:** Action sequences, product demonstrations, energetic scenes
**Direction:** Left-to-right or right-to-left

#### Crane Up (Epic, Establishing)
```
"Crane shot rising upward, camera elevates from ground level to high aerial view,
reveals full scale of environment, sweeping epic movement, god's eye reveal"
```
**When to Use:** Epic reveals, establishing shots, scale demonstration
**Height:** Low-to-high for maximum impact

#### Crane Down (Intimate Introduction)
```
"Crane shot descending from aerial view down to ground level, intimate introduction
to subject, smooth vertical descent, cinematic reveal"
```
**When to Use:** Opening shots, intimate introductions, scale-to-detail
**Speed:** Slow descent for dramatic effect

#### Pan (Scanning, Following)
```
"Smooth horizontal pan from left to right, camera rotates on fixed tripod point,
scanning environment, reveals space gradually, cinematic surveying"
```
**When to Use:** Environmental reveals, following horizontal action
**Speed:** Slow (dramatic) or Fast (whip pan for energy)

#### Tilt Up (Reveal, Power)
```
"Tilt up camera movement, starts at ground level then tilts upward to reveal subject,
dramatic vertical reveal, emphasizes height and power, heroic framing"
```
**When to Use:** Hero introductions, tall structures, power dynamics
**Speed:** Medium pace for controlled reveal

#### Orbit/Arc Shot (360Â° View)
```
"Orbital camera movement, camera circles around subject while maintaining focus,
360-degree product reveal, smooth arc motion, all angles visible"
```
**When to Use:** Product showcases, dramatic subject reveals, action scenes
**Angle:** Can be horizontal orbit or vertical gimbal orbit

#### Handheld (Documentary, Energy)
```
"Handheld camera aesthetic, subtle organic shake, documentary realism, human POV,
intimate and immediate feeling, natural movement"
```
**When to Use:** Documentary style, realism, energetic scenes, intimacy
**Intensity:** Subtle shake (professional) or pronounced (action)

#### Steadicam Glide (Smooth, Cinematic)
```
"Steadicam glide through environment, ultra-smooth floating motion, effortless
movement through obstacles, cinematic elegance, tracking subject fluidly"
```
**When to Use:** Cinematic commercials, smooth product tours, elegance
**Complexity:** Can combine with other movements

#### Push In (Dramatic Focus)
```
"Aggressive push-in camera movement, rapid forward motion toward subject,
creates urgency and drama, sudden intimacy, intense focus"
```
**When to Use:** Dramatic moments, urgency, intense emotion, climax
**Speed:** Fast for maximum impact

#### Pull Out (Revelation, Context)
```
"Slow pull-out revealing context, starts tight on detail then expands to show
full environment, narrative revelation, perspective expansion"
```
**When to Use:** Context reveals, storytelling twists, scale surprises
**Speed:** Slow to medium for dramatic effect

### Advanced Camera Techniques (2025)

#### Dolly Zoom (Vertigo Effect)
```
"Dolly zoom (Vertigo effect), camera dollies backward while zooming in simultaneously,
warped perspective creating disorientation, Hitchcock-inspired,
dramatic psychological effect"
```
**When to Use:** Psychological moments, realization scenes, dramatic impact
**Technical:** Requires precise timing of dolly and zoom

#### Whip Pan (Energy, Transition)
```
"Whip pan transition, extremely fast horizontal pan creating motion blur,
energetic scene transition, dynamic cut effect, music video aesthetic"
```
**When to Use:** High-energy edits, scene transitions, music-driven content
**Speed:** Maximum speed for blur effect

#### Rack Focus (Attention Shift)
```
"Rack focus shift, starts with foreground in sharp focus then shifts focus to
background subject, selective attention, shallow depth of field,
cinematic storytelling technique"
```
**When to Use:** Storytelling moments, attention direction, reveals
**Depth:** Requires shallow DOF (f/1.4-f/2.8)

## 5. Scene Composition & Depth

### Research Findings

**Source:** [AI for Video Depth of Field - ReelMind](https://reelmind.ai/blog/ai-for-video-depth-of-field-simulate-professional-focus)

> "AI-powered tools like Reelmind.ai are revolutionizing depth of field processing,
> enabling creators to simulate cinematic focus effects with unprecedented ease
> and precision"

**Source:** [Any-to-Bokeh Research (2025) - arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2505.21593v1)

> "A novel one-step video bokeh framework converts arbitrary input videos into
> temporally coherent, depth-aware bokeh effects using a multi-plane image (MPI)
> representation"

### Depth of Field Techniques

#### Shallow Depth of Field (Cinematic Isolation)
```
"Shallow depth of field, subject in sharp focus, background beautifully blurred,
bokeh effect visible, cinematic f/1.4 aesthetic, subject separation from environment,
creamy bokeh circles"
```

**Technical Specs:**
- Aperture: f/1.2 - f/2.8
- Lens: 50mm, 85mm, 135mm prime lenses
- Effect: Strong subject isolation, cinematic look

**Use Cases:**
- Portrait-style commercials
- Premium product showcases
- Emotional storytelling
- Luxury brand aesthetics

#### Deep Depth of Field (Environmental Context)
```
"Deep depth of field, everything in sharp focus from foreground to background,
environmental context visible, landscape photography aesthetic, f/11-f/16,
maximum information"
```

**Technical Specs:**
- Aperture: f/8 - f/22
- Lens: Wide-angle (24mm, 35mm)
- Effect: Maximum sharpness throughout frame

**Use Cases:**
- Real estate commercials
- Landscape/travel ads
- Environmental storytelling
- Wide establishing shots

#### Bokeh Effects (Artistic Background)
```
"Beautiful bokeh effect, out-of-focus background lights forming circular orbs,
creamy smooth blur, city lights at night creating bokeh balls,
aesthetic background separation, f/1.4 wide aperture"
```

**Bokeh Styles:**
- Circular bokeh (standard round aperture)
- Hexagonal bokeh (6-blade aperture)
- Octagonal bokeh (8-blade aperture)
- Swirly bokeh (vintage lens aesthetic)

**Use Cases:**
- Holiday/seasonal commercials (Christmas lights bokeh)
- Urban nighttime scenes
- Romantic aesthetics
- Premium product shots

### Composition Rules

#### Rule of Thirds (Balanced, Professional)
```
"Composition following rule of thirds, subject positioned at intersection point,
balanced frame, professional cinematography, golden ratio aesthetic"
```

#### Leading Lines (Directional Flow)
```
"Leading lines composition, architectural lines guide eye toward subject,
depth and perspective emphasized, vanishing point visible,
dynamic visual flow"
```

#### Symmetry (Formal, Powerful)
```
"Symmetrical composition, perfect balance, centered subject, formal and powerful
framing, Wes Anderson-inspired, architectural precision"
```

#### Negative Space (Minimalist, Emphasis)
```
"Abundant negative space, subject isolated with breathing room, minimalist
composition, emphasis through emptiness, clean and modern aesthetic"
```

## 6. Integrated Prompt Structures (2025)

### Advanced Product Commercial Template
```
[CAMERA]: [Movement] [Angle] shot
[SUBJECT]: [Product] [Action]
[LIGHTING]: [Setup] [Color temperature]
[COMPOSITION]: [Depth of field] [Framing rule]
[MOTION]: [Speed modifier] [Effect]
[TECHNICAL]: [Lens] [Resolution] [Style reference]
```

### Example: Luxury Watch Commercial
```
"Smooth dolly-in close-up shot. Luxury watch rotating on black velvet pedestal,
golden highlights catching on polished metal case. Rembrandt lighting with warm
key light (3200K) from camera left, creating depth and dimension. Shallow depth
of field (f/2.0) with creamy bokeh, watch face in sharp focus. Slow motion rotation
emphasizing craftsmanship. Shot on RED Komodo 6K with 85mm prime lens,
cinematic color grading, premium aesthetic."
```

### Example: Automotive Action Commercial
```
"Dynamic lateral tracking shot following sports car through mountain curves.
Car hugging tight turns at speed, tire smoke visible. Golden hour lighting (3500K)
with dramatic side lighting, long shadows emphasizing speed. Medium depth of field
showing car sharp, background motion-blurred. High-speed camera capturing detail.
Drone tracking shot maintaining perfect distance, Arri Alexa Mini aesthetic,
action cinema color grade."
```

### Example: Food & Beverage Macro
```
"Extreme close-up macro shot, cold craft beer poured into glass, effervescent
bubbles rising in slow motion. Dramatic side lighting (tungsten 3200K) creating
backlit amber glow through liquid, condensation beads visible on glass surface.
Ultra shallow depth of field (f/1.4), glass in sharp focus, background bar scene
beautifully blurred with warm bokeh. 100mm macro lens aesthetic,
```

## 7. Platform-Specific Optimizations

### Google Veo 3.1 Ultra (Primary)

**Strengths:**
- Excellent understanding of cinematography terms
- Native audio generation with ambience
- Strong temporal consistency
- Supports negative prompts for refinement

**Optimal Prompt Structure:**
```
[Establishing clause]. [Camera specification]. [Lighting detail].
[Motion descriptor]. [Technical aesthetic].
```

**Best Practices:**
- Use specific camera movement terms (dolly, crane, tracking)
- Specify color temperature in Kelvin when needed
- Reference professional cameras (Arri, RED) for aesthetic
- Include lighting setup descriptions
- Add "1080p" or resolution spec for quality

### Runway Gen-3 Turbo (Fallback)

**Strengths:**
- Fast generation (60-90 seconds)
- Strong motion consistency
- Excellent image-to-video capability
- Good with dynamic camera movements

**Optimal Prompt Structure:**
```
[Subject and action], [camera movement and angle], [lighting mood],
[additional technical details]
```

**Best Practices:**
- Keep prompts concise but descriptive
- Emphasize motion/action keywords
- Use "slow motion" for timing control
- Specify depth of field when needed
- Reference film aesthetic for quality

### Imagen 4 Ultra (Image Generation)

**Strengths:**
- 2K resolution output
- Excellent detail and sharpness
- Strong composition understanding
- Good with lighting descriptions

**Optimal Prompt Structure:**
```
[Subject description]. [Lighting setup]. [Composition style].
[Technical camera aesthetic].
```

**Best Practices:**
- Use positive emphasis only (no negative prompts)
- Specify depth of field desires
- Include lighting color temperature
- Reference camera/lens for aesthetic
- Mention "2K" or "high detail" for quality
